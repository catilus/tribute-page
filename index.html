Sir Arthur C. Clarke

Arthur C. Clarke on the set of 2001: A Space Odyssey .

  • 1917 – Born in December 16 in Minehead, Sommerset, England
  • 1936 – Moves to London. There, he becomes a pensions auditor of the Board of Education and shares a flat with fellow science-fiction writers
  • 1941–1946 – Serves in the Royal Air Force during World War II
  • 1948 – Writes The Sentinel, which will be the base for 2001: A Space Odyssey
  • 1950 – Writes Interplanetary Flight: An Introduction to Astronautics
  • 1951 – Writes The Exploration of Space
  • 1956 – Moves to Sri Lanka where he will live until his death
  • 1957 – Writes The Reefs of Taprobane after discovering the underwater ruins of a temple
  • 1968 – The movie 2001: A Space Odyssey is released and a few months later the book is published
  • 1982 – Writes 2010: Odyssey Two
  • 1986 – He is named Grand Master by the Science Fiction Writers of America
  • 1987 – Publishes 2061: Odyssey Three
  • 1989 – He is appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire
  • 1997 – Publishes 3001: The Final Odyssey
  • 2008 – Dies on March 19 in Colombo, Sri Lanka at the age of 90
